I built [Pika](https://superhighfives.com/pika) because I was tired of context-switching. Picking a colour off the screen shouldn't require opening a whole application, navigating a UI, and then copying a hex value. It should be instant.

So I made it instant.

---

What I didn't expect was what happened next.

---

## The thing about tools

There's a version of this story where I talk about Pika's features—the WCAG contrast checking, the colour name lookup, the keyboard shortcuts, the Raycast integration. And those things are genuinely useful. But that's not the interesting part.

The interesting part is what happens when the friction disappears.

When you stop thinking about *how* to do the thing, you start thinking about *what* you're doing. And that shift—from process to purpose—is where creativity lives.
